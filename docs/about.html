💊 AI for Drug Discovery

About DrugCLIP

A novel contrastive learning framework that aligns molecular and protein representations for accelerated virtual drug screening, achieving state-of-the-art performance across 14 benchmark datasets.

Our Mission

Accelerate drug discovery by replacing expensive, slow molecular docking with fast, accurate AI-driven virtual screening.

🎯

Mission Statement

Traditional virtual screening through molecular docking is computationally expensive and time-consuming, limiting the number of compounds researchers can evaluate. DrugCLIP brings contrastive learning — the same paradigm behind CLIP for images and text — to molecular biology, enabling rapid screening of billions of compounds at a fraction of the cost.

🔭

Vision

A drug discovery pipeline where AI can screen the entire chemical space (~10^60 drug-like molecules) in hours rather than years, identifying promising candidates for any protein target — including novel targets with no known ligands — through zero-shot transfer learning.

💡

Approach

DrugCLIP jointly trains molecular encoders (SMILES-based and graph-based) and protein pocket encoders using a cross-modal contrastive objective. This aligns the representation spaces so that molecules with high binding affinity are close to their target proteins in embedding space, enabling similarity-based screening.

Key Contributions

Advancing the state of the art in AI-driven drug discovery through novel representation learning.

🔗

Contrastive Molecular Alignment

Novel cross-modal contrastive objective that jointly trains SMILES and graph-based molecular encoders with protein pocket representations. Unlike prior approaches, DrugCLIP aligns both molecular modalities simultaneously for richer representations.

📊

Comprehensive Benchmark Suite

Standardized evaluation across 14 datasets spanning ADMET properties, binding affinity, toxicity, and activity prediction with unified metrics and protocols. First truly comprehensive comparison of molecular contrastive learning methods.

🧬

Multi-Scale Molecular Features

Hierarchical representation capturing atom-level, fragment-level, and global molecular semantics through a novel graph transformer architecture. This multi-scale approach captures both local chemical patterns and global molecular properties.

⚡

Zero-Shot Transfer Learning

Pre-trained representations transfer effectively to unseen protein targets without fine-tuning, enabling rapid screening of novel therapeutic areas where limited training data is available.

🎯

Binding Affinity Prediction

Direct prediction of binding affinities from contrastive embeddings, rivaling physics-based docking methods at 50x lower computational cost. Enables screening of ultra-large virtual libraries in real time.

🔄

Active Learning Pipeline

Integrated uncertainty-aware active learning loop that iteratively selects the most informative molecules for wet-lab validation, reducing experimental cycles and accelerating the drug discovery feedback loop.

Technology Stack

Cutting-edge deep learning infrastructure optimized for molecular and protein representation learning.

🧠

Deep Learning

PyTorch + PyTorch Geometric for graph neural networks, ESM-2 protein language model for protein representations, SMILES Transformer for molecular sequence encoding, and custom contrastive loss implementations.

🔗

Molecular Tools

RDKit for molecular feature extraction and cheminformatics, MoleculeNet benchmark suite for standardized evaluation, ChEMBL and ZINC databases for training data, and OpenBabel for molecular format conversion.

🏗️

Infrastructure

Multi-GPU training with distributed data parallel, WandB for experiment tracking and hyperparameter optimization, Docker for reproducible environments, and Hugging Face Hub for model distribution.

Development Roadmap

From research paper to production-ready drug discovery platform.

✅

Phase 1 — Research (Complete)

Novel contrastive learning framework design, training on 8.3M molecules, evaluation across 14 benchmark datasets, state-of-the-art results on MoleculeNet, and arXiv paper publication with open-source code.

🔄

Phase 2 — Scaling (In Progress)

Scaling to 100M+ molecule training set, incorporating 3D molecular conformations, protein structure integration with AlphaFold predictions, and improved zero-shot generalization through protein family-aware training.

📋

Phase 3 — Platform (Q2 2027)

GPU-accelerated screening API for production use, interactive web interface for non-technical researchers, integration with experimental design platforms, and automated active learning workflows for wet-lab validation.

🚀

Phase 4 — Ecosystem (2027)

Integration with PharmaSim for market analysis, DrugMind for target identification, and Harmes-Agent for safety profiling. Collaborative model training with pharmaceutical partners under federated learning framework.
