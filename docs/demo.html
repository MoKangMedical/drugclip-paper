How DrugCLIP Works

From molecular input to binding prediction in milliseconds.

1

Molecular Encoding

SMILES strings and molecular graphs are processed through the Graph Transformer encoder to produce 512-dimensional molecular embeddings.

2

Protein Encoding

Target protein sequences are encoded using the frozen ESM-2 protein language model, producing aligned protein embeddings in the shared space.

3

Similarity Scoring

Cosine similarity between molecular and protein embeddings yields binding affinity scores. Higher similarity = stronger predicted binding.

4

Interpretation

Attention maps highlight which molecular substructures and protein residues drive the prediction, providing mechanistic insights for medicinal chemists.
